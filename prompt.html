AI Playbook: Career Development Centre

Welcome to a New Era of AI-Assisted Work

This playbook transforms your interaction with LLMs like Gemini into a strategic partnership, unlocking the full potential of AI in student coaching, content creation, and administrative excellence.

The Three Core Principles

1. AI as Co-pilot, Not Autopilot

AI generates drafts and ideas, but your professional judgment is the final, irreplaceable authority. You are responsible for review and refinement.

2. "Garbage In, Garbage Out"

The quality of AI output depends entirely on your input. Invest time in crafting clear, detailed instructions for the best results.

3. Data Security: The Red Line

Never input Personally Identifiable Information (PII) of students. Protecting privacy is the highest priority and a non-negotiable rule.

Core Strategic Applications & Prompts

Exploring Unknown Topics (with Anti-Hallucination Measures)

The Situation:

A student from a niche field (e.g., 'Bio-Integrated Design') asks about career paths in a sector you've never heard of. You need to get up to speed quickly and reliably, without getting false information from the AI.

The Strategy:

Do not ask the AI for direct facts. Instead, instruct it to act as a research assistant and create a verifiable research plan for you. This forces the AI to outline a process rather than state potentially fabricated facts, giving you a safe and structured starting point.

Gold-Standard Prompt (Complex Version)
(R) You are a methodical research analyst with expertise in identifying reliable sources.
(C) I need to quickly understand a niche industry I know nothing about: 'The UK market for cultivated meat'.
(A) Your task is to create a research plan for me. IMPORTANT: Do NOT provide direct facts or statistics about the industry from your own knowledge.
(F) Instead, your output must provide the following three things:
1. Key Research Questions: List the 5-7 most important questions I would need to answer to get a comprehensive overview of this topic.
2. Effective Search Queries: For each question, suggest 2-3 specific, effective search queries I could use in Google, Google Scholar, or LinkedIn to find answers.
3. Reliable Source Checklist: List the types of sources I should prioritise (e.g., reports from the Food Standards Agency, articles in specific scientific journals, publications from industry associations like the Good Food Institute) and the types of sources I should treat with caution (e.g., anonymous blog posts, un-cited opinion pieces).
Simple Version
I need to research a new topic: '[Your Topic Here]'.

Please act as a research assistant. Don't give me facts directly.
Instead, create a simple research plan for me. I need:
1. Three key questions to ask.
2. Three good Google search terms to find the answers.
3. Three types of reliable sources to look for.

Workshop & Presentation Design

The Situation:

You need to create a workshop on a specific topic. You might be starting from a blank page or already have a structure in mind.

The Strategy:

Use AI as an instructional designer. Ask it to propose a logical flow and key learning objectives (The Architect). Or, if you have a structure, provide it to the AI and instruct it to generate the specific content for each section (The Builder).

Prompt Type A: The Architect
(R) You are an expert career educator...
(C) design a 60-minute workshop on 'Effective Networking for Beginners in the UK'...
(A) propose a complete workshop structure and a detailed outline...
(F) For each module: Module Title, Learning Objective, 3-4 bullet points...
(T) strategic, educational, and highly encouraging.
Prompt Type B: The Builder
(R) You are an expert digital branding strategist...
(C) preparing a 45-minute workshop for final-year Arts & Humanities students...
(A) Generate the content for a slide deck based on the specific structure below...
(F) Slide 1: Title, Slide 2: Agenda, etc...
(T) practical, engaging, and highly actionable.

Streamlining Internal Admin (Meeting Notes)

The Situation:

You have raw, messy notes from a meeting and need to turn them into a clear, actionable summary for your team.

The Strategy:

Instruct the AI to act as a highly efficient executive assistant. Provide the raw text and specify the exact format for the output, such as an email with sections for decisions and action items.

(R) You are a highly efficient executive assistant.
(A) Please convert my raw meeting notes below into a professional summary email.
(C) Raw Notes: "- careers fair next month, john says venue tbc..."
(F) The email should have a clear subject line and be structured with three sections: 'Key Decisions', 'Action Items (with owner)', and 'For Follow-Up'.

Tech & Software Help

The Situation:

You're struggling with a function in a software tool like Excel or Word. You either know what you want to achieve but not how, or you're unsure what a specific feature does.

The Strategy:

Use the AI as an infinitely patient tech support expert. Be explicit about your starting point and your desired outcome. Provide the context (what software, what data) and ask for the exact formula or a step-by-step guide.

"I Know the Goal, Not the Method"
(C) I am using Microsoft Excel. I have a list... Column A... Column B...
(A) I want to create a new column... that combines their names...
(F) Please provide the exact formula... and a brief explanation.
"I Know the Function, Not the Application"
(C) I am using Microsoft Word. I'm preparing a long report...
(A) I need to create an automated Table of Contents...
(F) Please provide a simple, step-by-step guide...

Advanced Technique: Teaching the AI to Self-Correct

AI models are often "confidently wrong" about specific code or syntax for niche tools like Mermaid.js. This case study shows you how to diagnose the error and teach the AI the correct rules in real-time.

Mermaid is a free, open-source platform for creating diagrams and charts from text. You can learn more here:

Mermaid.js Official Website | Mermaid Chart Dashboard | Getting Started (Many Examples)

Step 1: The Initial (Flawed) Attempt

First, we ask the AI to generate a mind map, but it produces incorrect code.

Prompt: (R) You are a strategic planner. (C) I am planning our annual 'STEM Careers Fair'. (A) I need you to brainstorm all the major components of this project. (F) Please generate the output as a Mermaid.js mind map code block.

Typical (Flawed) AI Response:

mindmap
  STEM Careers Fair Plan
      Phase 1: Planning
          Budgeting
          Employer Outreach
      Phase 2: Marketing
          Social Media
          Student Prep Workshops
      Phase 3: Execution
          On-the-day Staffing

Step 2 & 3: Identify Error & Provide a "Golden Example"

After seeing the code fails, you provide a working example as "ground truth." Find many official examples here.

Corrective Prompt: Here is a working example of a valid Mermaid mind map. Please acknowledge that you have seen it.

mindmap
  root((mindmap))
    Origins
      Long history
      ::icon(fa fa-book)
      Popularisation
        British popular psychology author Tony Buzan
    Research
      On effectiveness<br/>and features
      On Automatic creation
        Uses
            Creative techniques
            Strategic planning
            Argument mapping
    Tools
      Pen and paper
      Mermaid

Step 4: The Crucial "Rule Extraction" Prompt

This is the most important step. Ask the AI to become a syntax analyst, forcing it to learn from the correct example.

Gold-Standard Prompt: (A) Analyze the working Mermaid mind map example I just provided. Do NOT generate a new map. (F) Instead, list the specific syntax rules and principles required to create a valid map. Pay close attention to the root node, how children and grandchildren are created, and the use of indentation.

Expected AI Response (Analyzing the Rules):

Based on the example provided, here are the rules for creating a valid Mermaid mind map:
  • The code block must begin with the mindmap keyword.
  • The first line after the keyword defines the root node. It can be styled, for example, with ((text)).
  • Child nodes are created on new lines with one level of indentation (e.g., two or four spaces).
  • Grandchild nodes are created with a second level of indentation.
  • No special characters like hyphens (-) or asterisks (*) are used. The hierarchy is defined purely by indentation.

Step 5: The Final, Corrected Attempt

Now combine your original request with the newly established rules.

Gold-Standard Prompt: Excellent. Now, please regenerate the 'STEM Careers Fair Plan' mind map. (A) You MUST strictly follow the syntax rules you just extracted in your previous response. (F) Ensure the output is a valid Mermaid.js code block, using only indentation to create the hierarchy.

Correct AI Response:

mindmap
  ((STEM Careers Fair Plan))
    Phase 1: Planning & Strategy
      Budgeting
        Venue Costs
        Marketing Spend
      Employer Outreach
        Target List
        Invitation Tiers
    Phase 2: Marketing & Student Engagement
      Marketing Channels
        Social Media
        Departmental Emails
      Student Prep Workshops
    Phase 3: Event Logistics & Execution
      On-the-day Staffing
      Employer Welcome Packs
    Phase 4: Post-Event & Feedback
      Surveys
      Thank You Notes

Why This Works (The Key Takeaway)

This multi-step process is a powerful way to handle AI errors. By providing a correct example and forcing the AI to first analyze the rules before executing the task, you constrain its output and override its faulty training data. This is far more effective than simply saying "That's wrong, try again."

⚠️ Data Privacy & Compliance: The Hard Rules

This section is mandatory reading. Every team member must understand and adhere to these rules.

Absolutely Prohibited Input

  • Full names, student IDs, emails, phone numbers
  • Passport details, visa information, NI numbers
  • Any data that could identify an individual

Critical Action: Disable Model Training

In your AI tool's settings, navigate to Data Controls or Privacy Settings and ensure that "Chat history & training" is turned OFF. This is the most important step to protect privacy.
